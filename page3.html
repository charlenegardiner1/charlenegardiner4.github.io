Agriculture is critical to the future of sustainable development. Globally the entire sector is being challenged to produce more while being exposed to the changes in the environment.  Evidence of Climate Change have become more visible and the impacts are becoming more pronounced.  It is present in our local community and such changes affect farming.

Droughts, floods, hurricanes and storms, and all such natural phenomena affect a wide range of environmental factors and all activities related to agriculture, vegetation, human and wild life, debilitating local and national economies.

Many developing countries face severe economic, social and ecological threats from energy, food and water insecurity due to climate change and extreme weather risks. They also face risks from premature deaths due to pollution, poor water quality and diseases associated with a changing climate.

The Bahamas is a developing country with a need for improvement in technology.  There is a very important need for a technology that can tell us in advance what the environmental changes are and when they will occur. A most advanced weather sytem  presently being used is The Duplar Radar System. Thus, we are limited to the capabilities of this system. Other means are the Weather Channel and the internet search protocol.

For decades farmers have benefited from technological advances. We live in a world of interconnectiveness.The face of farming changed significantly over the past.  There are technolohical advances available and farmers are embracing the various new generation tools.

Developing countries such as The Bahamas must take seriously those technologies that are available and make the necessary sacrificies to obtain, maintain and operate them.

Earth Observation for Sustainable Development (EO4SD) is a new ESA initiative which aims at increasing the uptake of EO-based information in regular development operations at national and international level.

Green Growth and sustaianable development is another feasible concept that is rapidly being accepted.  Green growth relates to fostering economic growth and development while ensuring that natural assets continue to provide the resources and environmental services on which our well-being relies.

Green growth is not a replacement for sustainable development. Rather, it provides a practical and flexible approach for achieving concrete, measurable progress across its economic and environmental pillars, while taking full account of the social consequences of greening the growth dynamic of economies. The focus of green growth strategies is ensuring that natural assets can deliver their full economic potential on a sustainable basis.

Another innovation is Satellite Remote Sensing in Agriculture.  As extreme weather becomes more frequent causing several uncertainties, there isn’t much one can do about that, but one can provide for the storm on the horizon.

There are several new and sophisticated techniques  within this concept used for  mitigating the risks and forecasting crop yields .

One of these is remote sensing.  Remotely sensed imagery presents a cumulative picture of weather, climate data and cropping frequency and successful or otherwise report of farming.

Satellite imagery enables direct crop monitoring on a continuous basis over areas the size of villages, districts and states.
