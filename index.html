About Us

Our farm is located in a quiet section of South Abaco, Bahamas. We grow fruits vegetables and culinary herbs. One of the delightful aspects about farming is the anticipation that it brings. This year we anticipate much, much more......

Stewardship


Agriculture has become essential to life; the forest, the lake, and the ocean cannot sustain the increasing family of man; population declines with a declining cultivation, and nations have ceased to be with the extinction of their agriculture. We ensure proper land stewardship practices in order for the soil to be healthy for generations to come.  

Our Clients and Market

Argi-Business

Farm = Fiber + Food + Feul

Humus Farming is the natural way our Farm operates. Abaco has many marshlands. The farm is located in close proximity to a natural watershed. Inherent seasonal cycles of land, nearby rich humus compost, plant production, necessary soil health, the purity of the water, crop rotation and nature’s timing result in some dramatic differences from one season to the next that comprise the central activities of this agri-business.

Greenhouses and Proprogation Areas

Greenhouse work is a delightful way to begin to work the soil before it is time to work the land. Seed Starting is in the greenhouse. The greenhouse produce vegetable starts and herbs start for our feilds. Citrus tree seedlings are grown in our propogation area where they remain for a while. The farm produces many vegetable from seed and then the seedlings are transplanted into the feilds.

Vegetables

A variety of vegetables are grown in the garden. This selection include speciality items as well as tried and true favorites.  Some of the crop are sold in the local market.

Fruits

The sub-Tropical climate is a favourable host for the growth of a large variety of citrus fruits. The orchard contains small trees. There are avocados, guavas, pomegranates, lemons and sugarapples. These trees were planted about a year ago and will soon be bearing fruit.

Herbs

Culinary herbs are a featured of the spring garden and produce bountifully throughout the season in both the annual and perennial gardens. Thyme, Rosemary, Goat Pepper, Hot Pepper and Sweetbell Pepper are some of the native herbs that we grow.
